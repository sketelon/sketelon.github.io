Different Types of Words

The words in the language are divided into two main groups:

  • CONTENT WORDS
  • FUNCTION WORDS

CONTENT WORDS

Content words are words that deliver the content nouns.
nouns: sister, table, school…
verbs: go, run, swim…
adjectives: beautiful, red, clean…
adverbs: slowly, sometimes…

We usually stress content words in a sentence

FUNCTION WORDS

These are all the small words that connect content words. They are essential to create a gramatically correct sentence.
preposition: on, in, at…
verbs: is, am, are…
articles: a, an…
determiners: the, this, that…

Function words are unstressed, and not only that they’re unstressed, they were reduced to a point that it’s even not clear anymore

Example

The glass is on the table.

Stressed words are higher in pitch and longer. Every syllable takes me one step lower or higher.

reduce the vowels and connect the words together

Connected Speach

Think of a sentence as small units that are connected within themselves but are separated from one another. Don’t stop your voice or breath between the words you’d like to connect

1. VOWEL + CONSONANT

easy for almost all speakers (￣▽￣)"

2. CONSONANT + CONSONANT

don’t release the first consonant. Don’t try to be clear. Don’t try to say the words separately.

cheap prices
chea - prices

big gate
bi - gate

mixed nuts
mikstnuts

t + y/d +ｙ


get you
ge-chy'r

did you
di-djuw

3. CONSONANT + VOWEL

The consonant of the first word becomes the beginning of the next word that begins with a vowel.

work ethics
were-keh-thiks

finish it
fini-shit

4. VOWEL + VOWEL

If the first vowel is a back vowel, like uw or ow you will hear an intrusive sound.

you always
yuw-(w)aa-ways

who is it
who-wi-zit

go about
go-(w)a-bawt

If the first sound is an ‘e’ sound then we hear a ‘y’ sound.

see it
see-(y)it

she alway
shee-(y)aa-ways
